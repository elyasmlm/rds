District 1



"Between tradition and modernity, this district will allow you to discover magnificent places and enjoy the sublime view from the Trocadero square, while having time to learn about the history of these monuments"

Eiffel Tower



The Eiffel Tower is an iconic monument in Paris, France. It was built for the 1889 Universal Exhibition to celebrate the centenary of the French Revolution. The tower was designed by Gustave Eiffel and has become the symbol of France worldwide. It is approximately 324 metres high and is open to the public for tours. There are lifts to the top of the tower, where there is a breathtaking view of the city of Paris. The tower is also used for special events such as fireworks displays to celebrate various occasions.


École militaire



The École Militaire de Paris is a historic building located in the 7th arrondissement of Paris, France. It was built between 1751 and 1760 by the architect Jacques-Francois Blondel to be used as a school for young French noblemen who wanted to become officers in the French army. It was also used as a residence for Emperor Napoleon Bonaparte during his reign. The building is in neoclassical style and includes an inner courtyard, a chapel and classrooms. It is surrounded by a large park with gardens and statues. Today it is used by the Institut des Hautes Etudes Militaires, a training school for French army officers, as well as by other organisations and institutions. It is open to the public for guided tours.


Palais de Chaillot



The Palais de Chaillot is a building located on the Place du Trocadero, in the 16th arrondissement of Paris. It was built for the 1937 Universal Exhibition, on the site of the former Palais du Trocadéro. It is a remarkable example of Art Deco architecture, with its elegant style and terrace offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city, including the Eiffel Tower. The history of the Palais de Chaillot dates back to the 19th century, when the Palais du Trocadero was built for the 1878 Universal Exhibition. It was designed to house scientific and cultural exhibitions. However, after the exhibition, it was used for various purposes, notably as a museum of ethnography, then as the Musée des colonies, and finally as the Musée de l'homme. With the Universal Exhibition of 1937, it was decided to build a new building on the site of the Palais du Trocadéro, which would be more suited to the needs of modern France. The Palais de Chaillot was designed by the architect Jacques Carlu, Léon Azéma, Louis-Hippolyte Boileau and Léon Azéma. It is a remarkable example of Art Deco architecture, with elements of neoclassical and modernist styles. It was inaugurated on 6 July 1937 and has been used for many important exhibitions and cultural events. Today, the Palais de Chaillot is home to several cultural institutions, including the Musée de l'Homme, which explores humanities and cultures, and the Théâtre national de la Danse, which presents contemporary dance performances. It is also a place of celebration for major cultural events and national holidays. The terrace of the Palais de Chaillot offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city, including the Eiffel Tower and the Trocadero gardens. It is open to the public and is an interesting place for cultural visits in Paris. It is also a place of celebration for major cultural events and national festivities.











District 2



"The city of Paris is full of architectural wonders, and the Petit Palais, the Grand Palais, the Hôtel des Invalides, the Pont Alexandre III and the Place de la Concorde are proof of this. To visit these sites is to discover the history, art and culture of the city, and to immerse yourself in the soul of the City of Light."

Grand Palais



The Grand Palais is an emblematic building located on the Champs-Elysées in Paris. It was built for the 1900 Universal Exhibition, with the aim of showing the power and wealth of France at the time. It became a popular cultural venue, hosting many art and science exhibitions, as well as prestigious cultural events such as art fairs and fashion shows. The history of the Grand Palais dates back to the end of the 19th century, when France decided to build a large building for the 1900 Universal Exhibition. The project was entrusted to the architect Charles Girault, who designed an imposing building in a neo-classical style, inspired by Greek and Roman temples. Construction began in 1897 and took three years to complete.


Petit Palais



The Petit Palais is a historic building in Paris, France, located in the 8th arrondissement, built for the 1900 Universal Exhibition. It was designed by the architect Charles Girault and is considered an outstanding example of late 19th century neo-classical architecture. It was designed to be a place of culture and exhibition for the arts and sciences. Today, the Petit Palais is an art museum, housing a collection of European art from the Middle Ages to the late 19th century, with works by painters such as Monet, Renoir, Sisley and Degas. There is also a large collection of sculpture, ceramics, furniture and glassware. It is also used for temporary exhibitions and cultural events.


Hotel des invalides



The Hôtel des Invalides is a complex of buildings located in the 7th arrondissement of Paris, France. It was built in the 17th century to house wounded soldiers and veterans of the French army. It consists of a large number of buildings, including the Saint-Louis des Invalides church, the Army Museum, the Dôme des Invalides and the tomb of Napoleon Bonaparte. The Hôtel des Invalides is an important place for French military history, as it houses a large collection of arms, armour, uniforms, medals, decorations, memorabilia and documents relating to the history of the French army. There are also a large number of statues and memorials within the complex. Today, Les Invalides is a popular tourist attraction and place of pilgrimage for lovers of military history, as well as a place of celebration for important events related to the French army.



Bridge Alexander III



The Bridge Alexander III is a bridge in Paris, France, which connects the 8th and 16th districts of the city. It was named after Emperor Alexander III of Russia. It was built for the 1900 Universal Exhibition and is considered one of the most beautiful and prestigious bridges in the city. The bridge is made of steel and cast iron, with bronze decorations, glass lampposts and marble statues. It is topped by four statues representing France, Russia, science and art. There are also statues of nymphs and horses adorning the pillars of the bridge. It offers a breathtaking view of the Champs Elysées, the Ecole Militaire, the Eiffel Tower, and the Seine. The Bridge Alexandre III is a popular place for tourists and Parisians, who come to admire its luxurious decorations and to enjoy the panoramic views of the city. It is also a popular venue for weddings and special events.


Place de la Concorde



The Place de la Concorde is one of the most famous squares in Paris, located between the Champs-Elysées and the Seine. It was created in the 18th century to celebrate peace and friendship between nations. It is considered one of the largest public spaces in the city. The Place de la Concorde is surrounded by important buildings such as the Luxor Obelisk, which was donated to France by Egypt in 1829, and the Champs-Elysées gardens. It has also hosted important historical events such as the guillotine during the French Revolution, where many nobles and royalty were executed. Today, Place de la Concorde is a popular spot for tourists and Parisians alike, who come for its rich history, panoramic views of the city and numerous cultural and artistic events. It is also an obligatory stop for those visiting the Champs-Elysées and other tourist sites in the city.











District 3



"Discover the charm of Paris by visiting the architectural gems of Notre-Dame, the Sainte-Chapelle, the Louvre, the Palais de l'Institut and the Hôtel de la Monnaie. From the historic past to contemporary art, these iconic sites offer a unique and unforgettable cultural experience."

Palais du Louvre



The Palais du Louvre is a famous building located in Paris, France. It is known worldwide as one of the most important museums of art and civilization. It houses a collection of paintings, sculptures and artefacts from all eras, from antiquity to the contemporary period. It is one of the most visited museums in the world, attracting millions of visitors every year. The history of the Louvre Palace dates back to medieval times, when the building was constructed in 1190 as a fortress to protect the city of Paris. Over the following centuries, the building was expanded and transformed several times to become a royal palace. It was used as a residence for the kings of France until the 14th century. In the following centuries, the Palais du Louvre was used for various purposes, including as the national shop of medals and antiques, and later as the national museum of arts. In 1793, it was opened to the public as a national museum, offering a unique view of art and culture through the centuries. Today, the Palais du Louvre is a must-see for art and history lovers. It houses famous masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa, the Victory of Samothrace and the Sphinx of Tanis. It is also a popular place of entertainment, with temporary exhibitions, lectures and regular cultural events.


Palais de l'Institut



The Palais de l'Institut is a historic building located in Paris, France, in the 1st arrondissement. It was built in the 19th century to house the Académie française and the Institut de France. It is considered an outstanding example of late 19th century neoclassical architecture. Today, the Palais de l'Institut houses several academies, such as the Académie française, the Académie des Inscriptions et Belles-Lettres, the Académie des Sciences and the Académie des Beaux-Arts. It is also used for conferences, temporary exhibitions and cultural events. It is open to the public for guided tours upon reservation.


Hotêl de la Monnaie



The Hôtel de la Monnaie is a historic building in Paris, France, located in the 1st arrondissement. It was built in the 18th century to house the national mint of Paris. It is considered an outstanding example of late 18th century classical architecture. The Hôtel de la Monnaie is now a museum that houses a collection of objects related to the history of money in France, including coins, medals, tokens and banknotes. It is also used for temporary exhibitions and cultural events. It is open to the public for guided tours upon reservation.


La Sainte-Chapelle



The Sainte-Chapelle is a religious building located in Paris, France, in the 1st arrondissement. It was built in the 13th century to house the relics of the Passion of Christ, including the Crown of Thorns and the Shroud of Turin, given to Louis IX by the King of Jerusalem. It is considered an outstanding example of French Gothic architecture. The Sainte-Chapelle is famous for its colourful stained glass windows that cover almost the entire surface of its walls, creating an atmosphere of light. It is also remarkable for its high vertical structure which gives the impression of being in a heavenly space. Today it is used for religious ceremonies and concerts, and is open to the public for tours.


Notre-Dame de Paris



Notre Dame de Paris was begun in 1163 and completed in 1345. It is considered a masterpiece of Gothic architecture and was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1991. It has many artistic treasures such as stained glass windows, statues, paintings, organs and liturgical objects. It has also been the site of many important events in French history, including the coronations of the kings of France. The fire at Notre Dame in 2019 caused extensive damage to the wooden frame, roof, spire and bell towers. Fortunately, most of the artefacts and relics were saved, but the interior of the cathedral was severely damaged by smoke and water. Since then, restoration efforts have been undertaken to return the cathedral to its original state. The restoration of Notre Dame is a complex and expensive project that is expected to take several years to complete.
