The Monuments

Notre Dame

Access :


Access for people with disabilities :




  • The Notre-Dame de Paris Cathedral is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture dating from the Middle Ages. It is located on the Ile de la Cité in the heart of Paris and was built between 1163 and 1345. The cathedral is famous for its magnificent stained glass windows, sculptures and its facade adorned with statues. It is also known for its bell tower, the famous "belfry", which offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the city. Notre Dame Cathedral is an important place of worship for Catholics, but it is also a place of pilgrimage for tourists from around the world.

Sainte Chapelle

Access :


Access for people with disabilities :




  • The Sainte-Chapelle is a religious building located in Paris, on the Ile de la Cité. It was built in the 13th century to house the most sacred relics of Christianity, including the crown of thorns of Christ. The Sainte-Chapelle is a remarkable example of Gothic architecture, with its high ribbed vaults, walls decorated with colourful stained glass and statues of saints. It is best known for its 15 coloured glass windows depicting scenes from the Bible. The Sainte-Chapelle is a Catholic place of worship, but it is also open to the public for visits.

Eiffel Tower

Access :


Access for peaople with disabilities :




  • The Eiffel Tower is one of the most famous and iconic monuments in Paris. It was built for the 1889 Universal Exhibition and is 324 metres high. The Eiffel Tower has become the emblem of France and Paris, attracting millions of visitors every year. It is open to the public, offering panoramic views of the city from its three observation platforms. The Eiffel Tower is also a popular entertainment venue, with restaurants, bars and exhibitions. It is illuminated at night, providing a breathtaking spectacle for visitors. The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of world architecture.

Palais de Chaillot

Access :


Access for people with disabilities :




  • The Palais de Chaillot is a building located on the Place du Trocadero, in the 16th arrondissement of Paris. It was built for the 1937 Universal Exhibition, on the site of the former Palais du Trocadéro. It is a remarkable example of Art Deco architecture, with its elegant style and terrace offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city, including the Eiffel Tower. The Palais de Chaillot is home to several cultural institutions such as the Musée de l'Homme, which explores humanities and cultures, and the Théâtre National de la Danse, which presents contemporary dance performances. It is also a place of celebration for major cultural events and national holidays.
